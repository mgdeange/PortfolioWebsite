LANL Hackathon: Hazardous Material Handling Automation

Abstract

The team designed and built a system to automate the visual inspection and verification process of hazardous materials. This was part of the Devil's Invent Hackathon, a 3-day hacktathon sponsored by Los Alamos National Laboratories.

The Solution

While Los Alamos did say they wanted an automated solution, the team wanted to make sure that the operator was not completely removed from the equation. The focus was then on applying the operator in a way that they could be safer while still having an important job. The chosen concept was to create a system where the material would be handled by a robot arm and placed onto a roundtable that could be manually controlled by the operator so they could visually inspect the material without having to touch it themselves. The operator could then use an HMI to either approve or deny the material.

In the case of denial, the robot arm would send the material back for further production and future approval.

In the case of approval, the robot arm would take the material and place it gently into a barrel.
